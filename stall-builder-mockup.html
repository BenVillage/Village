About Hero
The Studio

A studio the size of a kitchen.

I learned to throw in Kyoto in 2017 and moved the practice to Berlin three years later. The work is slow on purpose — one gas firing a month, thirty to forty pots at a time.

Studio Gallery
Timeline
2017

First wheel in Kyoto

A three-month apprenticeship at a small family kiln outside Uji. Learned to center clay, trim pots, and wait.

2019

Back to Berlin

Rented a shared studio in Kreuzberg. Sold the first pots through friends and a shelf at a café.

2021

Own kiln

Found a narrow shopfront in Neukölln. Put a small gas kiln in the back. First solo firing that July.

2024

Once a month, on purpose

Switched to a single monthly firing. Fewer pots, more care. The kiln opens on the first Sunday.

Three Columns
i.

Thrown by hand

Every piece starts as a ball of local stoneware on a kickwheel.

ii.

Glazed from ash

Wood-ash and feldspar glazes mixed in small batches.

iii.

Fired once a month

A 14-hour firing to cone 10. The kiln opens on the first Sunday.
